Project Overview

Transforming climate research through advanced geospatial technology

Microsoft AUC Partnership

Developed under the Microsoft AUC (Azure University Credits) program, leveraging cloud computing resources for advanced climate analytics.

Jackson State University

Research conducted at JSU's Water Resources Lab, advancing geospatial climate research and education in the Mississippi region.

Google Earth Engine

Harnesses the power of Google's planetary-scale geospatial analysis platform for processing petabytes of Earth observation data.

Platform Capabilities

Comprehensive tools for climate data analysis and research

GeoData Explorer

Download and visualize Earth Engine datasets with interactive previews. Support for GeoTIFF, NetCDF, and CSV formats.

  • 54+ curated climate datasets
  • Interactive area selection
  • Multiple export formats
  • Direct download or Google Drive export

Climate Intelligence Hub

Calculate standard climate indices and analyze extreme events using ETCCDI standards.

  • 40+ climate indices
  • Temperature & precipitation analysis
  • Extreme events detection
  • Cloud-based processing

Interactive Mapping

Visualize data spatially and temporally with advanced mapping tools.

  • Draw study areas on map
  • Upload shapefiles/GeoJSON
  • Real-time data preview
  • Time series visualization

Time Series Analysis

Extract and analyze temporal patterns in climate data.

  • Trend analysis
  • Anomaly detection
  • Statistical summaries
  • Export to CSV/Excel

Extensive Data Catalog

Access to comprehensive climate datasets across multiple categories.

  • Precipitation data (11 datasets)
  • Temperature data (10 datasets)
  • Soil moisture (8 datasets)
  • Evapotranspiration (17 datasets)

Cloud Processing

Leverage Google Earth Engine's computational power for large-scale analysis.

  • Server-side processing
  • No local computation limits
  • Automatic scaling
  • Fast data processing
